My Publication

A Demonstration of mLoRA: A Parallelism-Efficient LLM Fine-Tuning System

Filali, S., Ye, Z., Li, D., Hu, Z., Lan, T., Sha, J., Zhang, S., Duan, L., Zuo, J., Lu, H., Zhou, Y., & Tang, M.

Submitted to PVLDB 2025 Demo Track · 2025

This paper demonstrates mLoRA, a system for parallel multi-LoRA LLM fine-tuning. The demo showcases the system on two database-domain tasks — Text2SQL and LLM4DP — achieving 30% faster optimization and 4–5× speedup over serial training...

This paper demonstrates mLoRA, a system for parallel multi-LoRA LLM fine-tuning. The demo showcases the system on two database-domain tasks — Text2SQL (parallel hyperparameter search achieving 30% faster optimization) and LLM4DP (4 parallel data preprocessing tasks with 4–5× speedup over serial training). This demo paper extends the full mLoRA research paper published at VLDB 2025. mLoRA introduces two core components: LoRAPP, a zero-bubble pipeline parallelism mechanism that leverages the independence of LoRA adapters to maximize GPU utilization across multiple GPUs, and BatchLoRA, a custom operator that consolidates multiple LoRA tasks into batched matrix operations to reduce kernel launch overhead. The system also includes a memory-aware task scheduler for efficient resource allocation. mLoRA achieves 30–45% faster training compared to existing parallel methods and has been deployed in production at AntGroup.
